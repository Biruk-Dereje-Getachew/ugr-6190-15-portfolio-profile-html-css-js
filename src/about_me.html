Personal Background

I was born in Addis Ababa, where I was first introduced to programming during a summer camp, sparking an interest in technology that would shape my academic journey. From a young age, I developed a competitive spirit, participating in science fairs, academic competitions, and spelling bees. These experiences fostered not only a strong sense of determination but also a love for problem-solving and innovation. I learned the importance of perseverance and strategic thinking, and these values have guided me ever since. Along the way, I’ve encountered challenges that have taught me valuable lessons in resilience, adaptability, and time management. Whether it was tackling difficult subjects in school or balancing multiple projects, I’ve always believed in the power of hard work and focused effort.

In everything I do, I prioritize function over form—seeking practical and effective solutions to problems. I take pride in my ability to stay organized and meet deadlines, and I enjoy finding ways to streamline processes to maximize productivity. I’m continually learning and seeking new opportunities to grow, both academically and personally. As I dive deeper into the world of software development, I’m inspired by the potential to solve real-world problems and make an impact. My journey so far has been shaped by my love for technology, my competitive nature, and my drive to improve myself with every challenge.

Education

Primary Education: attended Gibson Youth Academy for my primary education, a place that played a pivotal role in shaping my academic journey and building the foundation for my future success. It was at Gibson that I developed a strong sense of discipline and a passion for learning, participating in a variety of activities like science fairs and spelling bee competitions, which helped me hone my problem-solving skills and public speaking abilities. These competitions not only fueled my competitive spirit but also taught me the value of preparation, perseverance, and strategic thinking. The rigorous environment of Gibson Youth Academy encouraged me to push myself further and develop a deep appreciation for hard work and consistency.

Additionally, Gibson Youth Academy was where I learned most of my English. The school’s emphasis on language development helped me build strong communication skills, which have been crucial in both my academic and personal life. The ability to express myself clearly and effectively in English has opened doors for me, both in education and beyond. The academy instilled in me the importance of setting high standards and striving for excellence, which became a core part of my approach to education. I quickly learned how to manage my time effectively and prioritize my studies, skills that would continue to serve me well throughout my academic life. This focus and determination culminated in me finishing my primary education with a remarkable 99.9% percentile score in the leaving examination. Achieving this was not just a reflection of my academic abilities but also of the values of hard work and dedication that were ingrained in me during my time at Gibson. Looking back, my years at Gibson Youth Academy were foundational, equipping me with the tools I needed to excel and inspiring me to continue pursuing excellence in everything I do.

Secondary Education: attended Kotebe University of Education Menelik I Science Shared Campus for my secondary education, where I was consistently ranked in the top 5 of my class. While my primary academic focus was on subjects like math and science, I developed a strong interest in competitive programming, which became a hobby that I pursued alongside my studies. Through competitive programming, I sharpened my problem-solving skills and deepened my understanding of programming, but it was never the central focus of my academic work. My dedication to math and science, combined with my interest in programming, fueled my academic achievements.

Although I participated sparingly in extracurricular activities, my commitment to excelling in my studies remained a priority. This dedication paid off when I achieved a top 99.999% percentile score in the secondary leaving national exam. This accomplishment reflected not only my academic abilities but also the hard work and discipline I applied to my studies throughout my secondary school years. My time at Kotebe University of Education Menelik I Science Shared Campus solidified my passion for learning and laid the groundwork for my future endeavors in technology.

Higher Education: am currently studying Software Engineering at Addis Ababa University, where my academic journey has marked a transition toward greater independence and self-direction. The shift from structured learning in secondary school to the more autonomous environment of higher education has been both challenging and rewarding. My studies in software engineering have allowed me to deepen my understanding of technology while developing more advanced problem-solving and reasoning skills. The program has not only broadened my technical expertise but also sharpened my ability to think critically and tackle complex issues with confidence.

As I continue my education, I have been exposed to a wide array of theoretical and practical concepts in software development, pushing me to expand my knowledge and improve my technical abilities. The increased independence has also fostered a stronger sense of responsibility, encouraging me to take ownership of my learning and continuously strive for excellence in my academic and personal growth.

Competitive Programming: I have a strong interest in competitive programming, a hobby that challenges my analytical thinking and problem-solving abilities.
